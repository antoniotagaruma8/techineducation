The Educational Context

IES da Illa de Arousa

  • Target Audience: 4th Year ESO students at a critical transition stage.
  • Geographic Setting: A marine island community in Pontevedra with deep heritage.
  • Technical Challenge: Overcoming initial coding anxiety and syntax frustration.
  • Core Goal: Bridging the modern digital economy with local maritime roots.

Phase 1: Rapid Prototyping

  • Conduct deep research on the local Ría de Arousa ecosystem.
  • Leverage Google Stitch for text-to-design mockups.
  • Bypass manual wireframing to focus entirely on UX and usability.
  • Execute a "Design Handoff" with rival teams for peer QA testing.

The UI Prototype

06

Phase 2: UX Research & Design Handoff

  • Teams swap prototypes for a rigorous peer QA audit of usability.
  • Structured feedback forms evaluate visual hierarchy and accessibility.
  • Students adopt a "client vs. agency" dynamic to simulate real industry workflows.
  • Findings are consolidated into a written UX Report submitted to the original team.
07

Phase 3: AI-Assisted Coding

  • Transition to the Google Antigravity AI-powered IDE.
  • Direct AI agents to generate foundational HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Shift cognitive load from typing syntax to computational logic.
  • Students read, debug, refine, and manually integrate cultural texts.
