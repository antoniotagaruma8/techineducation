How this course works
During this course, you will design a technology
enhanced learning experience adapted to a specific
educational context. The project will be developed
progressively across the sessions, allowing you to
make informed pedagogical decisions and refine your
design step by step.
Final components:
o
3 project milestones
o
Final presentation
o
Personal reflective analysis
BONUSTRACK 1
Teaching and learning with
technology design project
The project will be completed in three milestones.
Each milestone builds on the previous one.
Submission schedule:
Milestone 1 →First Friday
Milestone 2 →Second Friday
Milestone 3 →Third Friday
On the final Friday, you will also present your project.
Final presentation
During the final session, you will present your project.
Presentation length: 5 minutes
Explain:
o
Educational context
o
Learning objectives
o
Teaching design
o
Implementation plan
You will also receive peer feedback.
Personal reflective analysis
Within 10 days after the final session, you will submit a 600-800
word reflective analysis.
In this reflection, you are expected to critically examine:
o
your learning and development throughout the project 
o
the main challenges encountered during the design process 
o
how your project evolved across the different stages 
o
potential improvements and future applications of your design 
You should also reflect on key concepts explored in the course, particularly:
o
the pedagogical integration of technology in teaching and learning 
o
the relationship between technology, context, and instructional design 
o
strategies for fostering student engagement and meaningful learning 
through technology
Project-Based Learning Cases
You will work individually on the development of your project.
Your task is to design a technology-enhanced learning
experience adapted to a specific educational context.
When developing your proposal, you are expected to consider:
o
the characteristics of the learners 
o
their educational needs 
o
the constraints and opportunities of the selected context 
You will choose one of the six educational contexts presented
below and use it as the framework for your project.
BONUSTRACK 2
Option 1. Complutum (Alcalá de 
Henares), Archaeological
workshop school
Introduction
The Roman archaeological site of Complutum is located near 
Alcalá University and is one of Madrid’s important historical 
heritage sites. Originally a Roman city, today it serves as a 
large archaeological park where visitors can explore ancient 
streets, mosaics and 2 original buildings.
Project focused on heritage education, archaeology, and 
community engagement.
Archaeological Workshop School at the Roman Site of 
Complutum.
As an educational project 
context, think about:
o
What kind of technology-enhanced learning experience could guide 
visitors through the Roman city of Complutum while helping them 
understand its historical and cultural significance?
o
How could you design activities that encourage learners to explore 
the archaeological site actively and engage with aspects of Roman 
daily life?
o
What role could technology play in supporting interpretation, 
interaction, or reflection during the visit (e.g., mobile devices, 
multimedia content, guided tasks)?
o
How could you structure the experience to promote collaboration, 
engagement, and meaningful learning in this specific context?
Website: www.complutum.com. In Spanish, use browser translation.
Option 2. Adult Education Centre, 
CEPA Río Sorbe (Guadalajara)
Introduction
The CEPA Río Sorbe is a public Adult Education Centre 
in Guadalajara focused on basic education, digital 
literacy, professional orientation and lifelong learning 
for adult learners. It offers programmes such as 
secondary education for adults and support for 
professional transitions. 
Project focused on lifelong learning, digital literacy, and 
social inclusion
Website: Adult Education Centre - CEPA Río Sorbe
For this project, think about:
o
What kind of technology-enhanced learning experience could 
support adult learners in developing practical skills for everyday 
life or professional contexts?
o
How could you design activities that are relevant, flexible, and 
meaningful for students with diverse backgrounds, experiences, 
and learning goals?
o
What role could technology play in facilitating access, 
personalization, and autonomy, considering the specific needs 
and schedules of adult learners?
o
How could you promote collaboration, motivation, and active 
participation in a context where learners may have very different 
levels of engagement and availability?
Website in Spanish, use translation tools to translate key pages and understand 
programmes and mission.
Option 3. Rural nursery school in El 
Pobo de Dueñas (Guadalajara)
Introduction
El Pobo de Dueñas represents a small rural educational 
context. Although the specific school site is provided in 
Spanish, your project should focus on the challenges and 
opportunities of rural education, including access, 
community involvement and digital connectivity.
Project focused on rural education, digital inclusion, and 
community development.
Rural nursery school in the depopulated area of El Pobo de 
Dueñas (España vaciada)
As a design challenge, think about:
o
How could you design a simple, age-appropriate technology-
enhanced learning experience that supports early childhood 
learning through exploration, curiosity, and play?
o
What kind of playful activities could help develop early learning 
skills such as colours, shapes, sounds, basic language, or sensory 
awareness?
o
How could technology (or low-tech alternatives when appropriate) 
be used to create a safe, meaningful and developmentally suitable 
learning experience for very young children?
o
How could you design activities that encourage movement, 
imagination, creativity, and interaction, respecting early childhood 
learning principles?
Website: linked through a Spanish education directory. 
Use translation tools for context.

Option 6. Rural School CRA

Introduction
The Government of La Rioja has produced a
