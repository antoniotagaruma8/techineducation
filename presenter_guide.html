Master Presenter Guide Stage Directions & Script

Instructions: Use this guide alongside presentation.html. Every time you see a CLICK / NEXT badge, press Spacebar or Right Arrow to advance. Follow the orange stage directions to command the room. Total spoken length: ~4 to 4.5 minutes (~500 words), within the 5-minute maximum.

Slide 1: Title Card
0:00 - 0:30
START [Stand center stage, confident posture. Let the title fade in behind you.]
"Good morning. My project will explore teaching web development with AI tools in the classroom at IES da Illa de Arousa. We will use generative AI to help students build real websites for their community, connecting them to their local maritime heritage without getting stuck on syntax."
Slide 2: Educational Context
0:30 - 1:00
CLICK / NEXT [Walk towards the left side of the screen.]
"Our context is the IES da Illa de Arousa, targeting 4th-year ESO students (15-16 years old). This coastal island community has strong maritime roots, and our goal is to merge that rich heritage with relevant, modern digital skills."
[Make direct eye contact.]
"The main technical challenge will be overcoming their initial coding anxiety and frustration with syntax. Our solution will use AI to bypass those barriers, empowering them to build a sustainable tourism web portal that reinforces their local maritime identity."
Slide 3: Learning Objectives
1:00 - 1:30
CLICK / NEXT [Use hands to visually weigh the two sides.]
"Technically, students will master building websites with HTML and CSS, learn professional UI/UX design, and develop advanced AI prompt engineering skills to generate code and layouts."
[Point to Conceptual Growth column.]
"Conceptually, they will understand the socio-economic dynamics of sustainable tourism, learn how to ethically verify AI outputs through rigorous QA, and practice collaboration in a simulated digital agency workflow."
Slides 4 & 5: Teaching Design (Phase 1)
1:30 - 2:15
CLICK / NEXT [Slide 4: Text]
[Engage the audience directly.]
"The teaching design will mimic a real-world digital agency. After initial research, teams will enter Rapid Prototyping. Instead of basic sketching, students will use 'Google Stitch.' They will engineer text prompts to generate professional, culturally accurate UI wireframes."
CLICK / NEXT [Slide 5: UI Image]
[Gesture open-handed towards the devices.]
"Here you can see a sample of the kind of website prototype they will create. By skipping initial design friction, they will focus entirely on usability and cultural representation, culminating in a peer 'Design Handoff'—which is the industry term for when designers officially pass their finished visual mockups over to the development team to be built."
Slide 6: Teaching Design (Phase 2)
2:15 - 2:50
CLICK / NEXT [Walk towards the audience.]
"Teams will then swap prototypes for a rigorous peer QA audit. They will adopt a 'client vs. agency' dynamic, evaluating usability and visual hierarchy to compile a formal UX Report for the original team."
Slide 7: Teaching Design (Phase 3)
2:50 - 3:30
CLICK / NEXT [Pace slightly forward.]
"Next, they will transition into the Google Antigravity IDE. Rather than typing every tag manually, students will act as Lead Developers, directing AI agents to generate foundational HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The focus will shift entirely to computational logic."
[Gesture a shift in perspective.]
"They will read AI-generated code, verify functionality, debug layout breaks, and integrate their authentic cultural text to bring the portal to life."
Slides 8 & 9: Pedagogical Foundation
3:30 - 4:20
CLICK / NEXT [Slide 8: Text]
"Pedagogically, this will synthesize Project-Based Learning and Place-Based Education. This will propel us to the 'Redefinition' level of the SAMR model. By using AI as a co-pilot, we will bypass the syntax barrier that normally frustrates beginners. Students will become system architects, not just typists."
CLICK / NEXT [Slide 9: TPACK Diagram]
"As you can see on the left, this project will hit the 'Redefinition' peak of the SAMR model, allowing students to tackle complex web tasks that were previously inconceivable for them. On the right, this approach perfectly balances the TPACK framework: merging AI Technology, Project-Based Pedagogy, and local Tourism Content into a single, cohesive experience."
Slide 10: Implementation Plan
4:20 - 5:00
CLICK / NEXT [Count off roles on fingers.]
"Our implementation plan will transform the classroom into a co-working space. I will shift from lecturer to 'Technical Director,' circulating to unblock teams. Students will rotate through professional roles: Project Manager, UX Designer, Lead Developer, and Content Strategist."
[Ground yourself.]
"To ensure the project remains deeply authentic, students will conduct real-world interviews with the local fishing community to ground their digital work in reality."
Slide 11: Assessment & Conclusion
5:00 - 5:40
CLICK / NEXT [Stand tall, center stage.]
"Finally, assessment will be continuous. Formatively, I will review their daily 'Prompt Logs.' Summatively, their final product will be evaluated via a 4-dimensional rubric covering AI prompting, code architecture, UX design, and cultural authenticity."
[Smile warmly.]
"In conclusion, trusting students with professional AI tools and grounding their work locally will drive massive intrinsic motivation. We will teach them computational thinking, ethical design, and immense pride in their heritage. Thank you."
Slide 12: Questions
5:40+
CLICK / NEXT [Return to center stage. Open posture, hands relaxed.]
"Thank you very much. I'm happy to take any questions you may have about the project, the methodology, or the tools we plan to use."
